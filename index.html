5-Minute Mindfulness

Mindfulness is the practice of paying attention to the present moment on purpose, without judgment. It's a simple way to pull yourself out of "autopilot" mode and reduce stress. You don't need a quiet room or a yoga mat. You can do it right where you are.

The 5-Minute Reset:

  1. Set a timer: Set a 5-minute timer on your phone.
  2. Get comfortable: Sit in your chair. Place your feet flat on the floor, unclench your jaw, and let your shoulders drop. You can close your eyes or just rest them on a point in front of you.
  3. Focus on your breath: Don't try to change it. Just notice it. Feel the air coming into your nose. Feel your chest or belly expand. Notice the air leaving your body.
  4. Notice your thoughts: Your mind *will* wander. That's what minds do. When you notice it wandering (to your to-do list, to a class, to what's for dinner), just gently say "thinking" to yourself and bring your focus back to your breath.
  5. Be kind to yourself: The goal isn't to have zero thoughts. The goal is to just *notice* your thoughts and come back to your breath. Every time you come back, you are succeeding.

That's it. When the timer goes off, take one more deep breath and continue with your day.

Beating Procrastination

We procrastinate because the task feels too big, too boring, or too stressful. The secret isn't "motivation"—it's building momentum. The easiest way to do that is with the "10-Minute" trick.

The 10-Minute Trick:

  1. Pick ONE thing: Don't look at your whole to-do list. Just pick the *one* thing you are avoiding the most.
  2. Set a 10-minute timer: Commit to working on that one task, and *only* that task, for just 10 minutes. Anyone can do 10 minutes.
  3. The "No-Distractions" Rule: For those 10 minutes, put your phone on silent and in another room. Close all other tabs. Give it your full focus.
  4. When the timer goes off... you have a choice:
    • If you're in the groove, great! Keep going. You've already overcome the hardest part (starting).
    • If you're still hating it, that's fine too. Stop. You're done for now. You've still made 10 minutes of progress, which is infinitely more than zero.

More often than not, just starting is enough to break the mental barrier. You'll find yourself working for 20 or 30 minutes without even realizing it.

Dorm Room Workouts

Stuck inside? You can still get a great workout in a small space with zero equipment. Try this simple circuit. Do each exercise for 45 seconds, rest for 15 seconds, then move to the next. Repeat the whole circuit 3 times.

The Circuit:

  • Bodyweight Squats: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart. Lower your hips as if sitting in a chair, keeping your chest up and back straight.
  • Push-ups (or Knee Push-ups): Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels (or knees). Lower your chest toward the floor and push back up.
  • Glute Bridges: Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Squeeze your glutes to lift your hips off the floor until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees.
  • Plank: Hold a push-up position, either on your hands or your forearms. Keep your core tight and don't let your hips sag.
  • Jumping Jacks: A classic for a reason! Gets your heart rate up. If you need to be quiet, do "step jacks" instead (stepping one foot out at a time).

This whole workout takes about 15 minutes, gets your heart pumping, and helps clear your mind—all without leaving your room.
